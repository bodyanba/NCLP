Who should apply?


In this its inaugural year, the NCLP aims to select 20 participants from Oman’s public sector and 20 participants from Oman’s private sector.


Diversity

A diverse learning cohort is an important part of the Program and we strongly encourage a cross-section of high-potential nationals representing public and private sectors, functions and regions in the Sultanate to apply.


Admission

Admission to the NCLP is based on the applicant’s professional achievement and leadership potential.

Applications to the program will be evaluated through a transparent, rigorous and merit-based process designed and managed by our independent and world-class international human resources consulting partner.

Application Process


Important Dates in the Application & Selection Process

  • You can apply online for the NCLP from 12:00am on 25th June until 11.59pm on 8th July. It is not possible to apply outside of these dates.
  • From 9th July to 30th July applications will be screened and 80 candidates will be shortlisted for assessment and interview.
  • Shortlisted candidates will be informed on 31st July and invited for assessment and interview.
  • Assessment and interview will take place between 2nd September and 12th September.
  • Announcement of the 40 candidates selected to take part in the NCLP will be made on 23rd September.
  • NCLP Engagement Module One is from 15th to 17th October inclusive.

Step 2

In Step Two:

  • You complete the Application Form.
  • You must complete all sections of the Application Form.
  • This should take between 45 - 90 minutes.
  • You will also be required to write responses to a series of questions.
  • Please write your answers in the spaces provided to a maximum of 100 words per response.
  • The questions will test your motivation and help assess your potential for joining the NCLP.

Think carefully about your short answer responses and use them to build your case for why you should be selected to participate in the NCLP. You should highlight your commitment to the Program and how you will apply learning back to future public-private sector collaboration. Concrete examples will help your case significantly.

When you submit your completed application form, you will need to include a CV.

  • Click here for the template that you should use for your CV.
  • Prepare your CV in advance and save it in PDF format.
  • It should be a maximum of four pages in length.
  • Remember - your CV is an important written representation of your profile and provides vital information that will help the National Programs Team evaluate your application.
  • Your CV should outline your key responsibilities and achievements to date, but should not be a standard job description of the role.
  • It should provide clear supporting evidence for your responses to the questions.

As well as your CV, you will also need to provide the following (as PDF files):

  • A scanned copy of your valid Omani National ID card (private and public sector applicants).
  • A scanned copy of your government work card (public sector applicants).
  • A support letter from your CEO/Chairperson (private sector).
  • A support letter from your Under Secretary/Minister (public sector).
  • Click here for the template that should be used for your Letter of Support.

When you have completed the Application Form and have your CV and supporting documentation ready, click Submit to complete Step Two of the application process.

Notes

  • You may save your application and come back later to complete it at any time.
  • Please submit your completed application and question responses, CV, and supporting documentation by 11:59pm on 8th July.

What’s Next?

Application Tracking

  • You will receive an automated email acknowledging receipt of your submission from [insert correct email address].
  • This email will include your application reference number.
  • You must quote this number in any future communications with the National Programs Team.
  • Check your junk / spam email folders for emails throughout the application process in case some of our important messages have been filtered out.
  • In the unlikely event that you do not receive an email acknowledgement, please contact us at [insert correct email address].
  • You may check the status of your application at any time by clicking here or visiting the Apply page and entering your application number in the Track Application Status section.

After That?

  • From the applicants who have submitted complete applications and met the selection, 80 will be short-listed to progress to Step Three and invited to attend the Assessment Centres.
  • By 31st July you will receive an email advising if your application has been short-listed for the Program or not.
  • If you are successfully nominated, the email will provide instructions for Step Three of the process.
  • We are sorry but we are unable to provide individual feedback for applicants who are unsuccessful at Step Two of the Admissions process.

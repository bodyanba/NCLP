Cambridge Leadership Consortium (UK)

The Cambridge Consortium consists of key organizations and university institutes from the world-class innovation ecosystem in Cambridge, UK. The consortium includes St Johns Innovation Centre, The Institute for Manufacturing, the Moller Centre and the Cambridge Institute for Sustainability Leadership.

Collectively, the consortium works with global organisations, governments and NGO’s to support collaborative leadership in a wide range of sectors and initiatives.

The program is designed and delivered by the Cambridge Consortium, which includes:

St Johns Innovation Centre

Europe’s first Innovation hub, created by St John’s College, Cambridge University in 1987. With significant experience in giving practical support and training to public sector and private sector innovators across the world.

Visit Website

Institute for Manufacturing (IfM)

As part of the Department of Engineering within the University of Cambridge, the IfM has an extensive track record in developing leaders to take key roles and value generation in the context of 4IR technologies.

Visit Website

Cambridge Institute for Sustainability Leadership (CISL)

This globally renowned Institute advises, trains and supports leaders in corporate, government and international bodies to develop business models and policy approaches that are sustainable and effective into the future.

Visit Website

Moller Institute, Churchill College (MICC)

The Moller Institute is a world-class, residential leadership development centre with extensive experience in enabling leaders across the globe to develop the cognitive, behavioural and emotional skills to lead through complex times.

Visit Website

Emergenetics International

With their UK head office located in Cambridge, Emergenetics brings robust and contemporary brain science knowledge into the program and provides support that will underpin the success of the project teams within the program

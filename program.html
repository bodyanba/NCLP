Program Overview

The NCLP is an eight-month learning process that aims to equip a community of public and private sector collaborative leaders with the skills, attitudes and capabilities to tackle the cross-public and private sector challenges and opportunities facing the Sultanate.

On graduation from the NCLP, participants will be admitted to membership of the Oman Business Forum (OBF). This exclusive community of leaders is dedicated to fostering collaboration between the public and private sectors as well as facilitating and enhancing the sharing of ideas, knowledge and best practice between the two groups.

Learning Context

To maximize the impact of the learning, the NCLP is delivered in a variety of contexts and locations in Oman and overseas.

Learning Context

For the 2018 – 2019 cohort, learning will be delivered in:

  • Singapor
  • IMD in Switzerland
  • Henley Business School UK

In Oman, sessions will be conducted in both Muscat and Jebel Akhdar.

Time Commitment

The Program requires a considerable time commitment.

Time Commitment

  • Participants will be required to attend a total of five modules (equivalent to 23 days) over a period of eight months.
  • Twelve of these days will be spent outside of Oman (not including travel time).
  • Between modules, participants are also required to work extensively on Group Projects. Approximately 20 additional days will need to be allocated to this by the participant.

Orientation Session

The upfront Engagement Module establishes trusted and trusting relationships between public and private sector participants. Using a range of community building mechanisms, the Engagement Process establishes a level playing field and a common language in order to build a collaborative and supportive environment.

1 / Engagement

Placing the participant in an unfamiliar environment, this challenging learning methodology catalyses new thinking about a range of leadership, management, and professional approaches. During the NCLP, participants will be immersed in the 4IR environment, facilitating their understanding of the future world of work. Additionally, experiential learning will introduce participants to examples of effective, as well as less effective, public-private collaboration.

The immersion and experiential learning will take place in the international environment.

2 / Immersion

A powerful and thought-provoking setting, the NCLP classroom delivers the tools, techniques, skills and capabilities required for Collaborative Leaders. This is designed around a strategic leadership journey.

3 / Mega-Dive

Highly innovative in approach, Module Three will develop a large public and private sector community equipped with an understanding of 4IR, its implications for Oman, and the resultant competitiveness criteria.

4 / Leadership Development & Application

Focused on strategic public-private opportunities or challenges related to Oman’s competitiveness criteria, the collaborative projects provide the opportunity for cross sector groups to apply learning outcomes back to the workplace and to the national agenda of the Sultanate..

Participants will hold project presentations throughout the Program, concluding with a formal final presentation to senior stakeholders.

MegaDive & Application Process

An integral part of all the NCLP is the application of learning to real Omani competitiveness opportunities and challenges. The Project Application Process will be focussed on addressing very practical competitiveness criteria, some of which will be strategic and some of which will be tactical.

The first step in this is the Mega Dive which brings together all alumni and current participants of the National Programs – the NCP, the NLCP, the National Youth Program (NYP) as well as the NCLP - for an intensive high energy design thinking day about competitiveness. The priorities generated from this gathering will form the basis of the Project Application Process going forwards.

3 / The MegaDive

Muscat, Oman

The focus of this module is to deliver a better understanding of the criteria driving competitiveness to a much broader community.

With approximately 200 participants, the MegaDive is a one-day event that draws upon the participants from the NCLP, as well as alumni from the National CEO Program, (NCP), alumni from the National Leadership & Competitiveness Program (NLCP), representatives from the Youth Program (NYP), and from the PPP community.

A high-energy exercise in idea generation, the MegaDive that will bring fresh perspectives to the competitiveness opportunities (and challenges) facing the Sultanate today and three to five years into the future. It is a process for structuring good conversations that will encourage active collaboration among key stakeholders shaping the competitiveness of Oman.

4 / Leadership Development and Application

Henley-on-Thames, UK and Lausanne, Switzerland

The first part of this module (and Module Five) is designed to equip participants with the strategic and tactical leadership skills and capabilities both to drive the competitiveness of Oman as well as build the Public Private Sector collaborative leadership community.

This seven-day module (including travel between UK and Switzerland) involves subject areas such as: strategic thinking, future trends and scenarios, understanding your leadership profile, strategic communication, creativity and innovation.

The second part of Module Four will be spent progressing the Application Projects to full implementation. Fostering participants’ entrepreneurial mindset - building on and refining the ideas developed during the MegaDive - Project Groups will define the value they create in terms of increased competitiveness for the Sultanate of Oman. Groups will develop suitable business models for these ideas, build prototypes, and plan the tests required to be conducted in the marketplace.

4 / Leadership Development and Application

Muscat, Oman

The first part of this seven-day module is focused upon developing strategic implementation and tactical leadership. As such, this highly practical few days will involve exploring themes including change management, strategic and tactical decision-making, influencing without authority, reputation (national, organisational and personal) alongside personal development planning for the future.

The second part of Module Five allows Groups to refine the business case underpinning their Projects, and so make preparation for effective implementation, and helps Groups and participants finalise the narrative to what will be used to communicate Projects ideas with impact. The Module will end on a high note with presentations to key national and international stakeholders.
